Geography

Olympia sits at the gateway to the Olympic National Park. North of the City is Puget Sound, a deep inlet of the Pacific Ocean. The inlet stretches over one hundred miles and contains a chain of islands along the way. Puget Sounds is home to several harbors that include Tacoma, Seattle, Port Townsend, and Seattle.

History

Located on Budd Inlet at the south end of Puget Sound, lives the capital of the State of Washington; Olympia. The city was originally named Smithfield in 1851, but was renamed after the Olympic Mountains. The City became the State seat in 1853.
