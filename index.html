Geography

Washington State is in the Pacific Northwest Region of the continental United States. It is surrounded by he States of Oregon (south) and Idaho (east), as well as Canadian providence of British Columbia(north). o the west lies The Pacific Ocean.

History

The state joined the Union on November 11, 1889, becoming the 42nd state at the time. The capital of the State is Olympia, named after the Olympic Mountains. The State was named after the United States first president, George Washington. This makes Washington State the only state to be named after a Unites States president.

Animals and Plants

Washington State has healthy forest presence. Approximately half of the land area is covered by forest. The major tree species that inhabit the state include western red cedar, Douglas fir, ponderosa pine and hemlock. Animals such as the cougars, bears, deer, elk mountain goats call this state home. The coast is home to several species of whales (killer whales, humpback and grey), and the rivers are home to many types of fish (trout, bass, sturgeon and salmon).
